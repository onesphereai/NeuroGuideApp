## PHASE 1: AUSTRALIA (PRIMARY MARKET)

### A. Filing Strategy

**1. Australian Provisional Patent Application**

**Timeline:** File immediately upon US provisional filing
- **Authority:** IP Australia
- **Filing Method:** Direct filing or Paris Convention route
- **Cost:** ~AUD $150 provisional filing fee
- **Benefits:**
  - 12-month priority date
  - Lower initial cost than complete application
  - Time to refine claims based on market feedback
  - Can delay examination costs

**2. Australian Complete Patent Application**

**Timeline:** Within 12 months of provisional
- **Filing Fee:** ~AUD $330
- **Examination Request:** Can be delayed up to 5 years, but recommend within 12 months
- **Examination Fee:** ~AUD $490
- **Total Initial Cost:** ~AUD $1,000-2,000 (including attorney fees)

**3. Australian Innovation Patent (Alternative)**

**Fast-Track Option:**
- **Timeline:** 1-6 months to grant
- **Term:** 8 years (vs 20 for standard patent)
- **Lower Novelty Bar:** "Innovative step" vs "inventive step"
- **Cost:** ~AUD $580 filing + examination
- **Best For:** Quick market protection while standard patent pending

**Recommendation:** File BOTH standard patent and innovation patent
- Innovation patent provides immediate protection for launch
- Standard patent provides long-term protection

### B. Australian Market Considerations

**1. Market Size & Opportunity**

- **Autism Prevalence:** ~1 in 70 Australians (164,000 autistic people)
- **ADHD Prevalence:** ~5-8% of children (~300,000)
- **Total TAM:** ~500,000 neurodivergent children
- **Healthcare Market:** $220 billion, 10% GDP
- **Digital Health Adoption:** High - 85% smartphone penetration

**2. Regulatory Requirements**

- **TGA Registration:** May be required if making therapeutic claims
  - **Device Classification:** Likely Class I (lowest risk)
  - **Self-Declaration:** Can self-declare if Class I
  - **Cost:** ~AUD $5,000-15,000 if regulatory review needed

- **NDIS Alignment:** National Disability Insurance Scheme
  - **Opportunity:** Get approved as NDIS-funded assistive technology
  - **Benefit:** Government funding for families
  - **Process:** Apply for inclusion in NDIS Price Guide

**3. Privacy Compliance**

- **Australian Privacy Act 1988:**
  - ✅ Already compliant (on-device processing)
  - ✅ Australian Privacy Principles (APPs) met
  - Advantage: Privacy-first design avoids cross-border data issues

- **My Health Records Act:**
  - Not applicable (not a health records system)
  - Benefit: Avoids complex integration requirements

**4. Patent Enforcement**

- **Litigation Costs:** AUD $500k-2M for full patent litigation
- **Federal Court Jurisdiction:** Patents heard in Federal Court
- **Infringement Remedies:**
  - Injunctions (common and effective)
  - Damages or account of profits
  - Legal costs recovery

**5. Strategic Partnerships**

**Potential Australian Partners:**
- **Autism Spectrum Australia (Aspect):** Largest autism service provider
- **Telethon Kids Institute:** Leading autism research
- **Royal Children's Hospital Melbourne:** Clinical validation
- **NDIS Partners:** Provider network integration

## ENFORCEMENT STRATEGY

### Defensive vs. Offensive IP Strategy

**Defensive (Recommended Initially):**
- **Goal:** Prevent competitors from copying innovations
- **Approach:**
  - Publish technical details to establish prior art
  - Patent key innovations to block copycats
  - Use IP as barrier to entry
- **Cost:** Lower (filing + maintenance only)

**Offensive (If Infringement Detected):**
- **Goal:** Enforce against infringers
- **Approach:**
  - Cease and desist letters
  - Licensing negotiations
  - Litigation if necessary
- **Cost:** High ($200k-2M per country for litigation)

**Recommended Approach:**
- Defensive strategy for first 3 years
- Monitor market for potential infringers
- Build licensing revenue model (Year 4+)
- Only litigate if major competitor infringes

## FREEDOM TO OPERATE (FTO) ANALYSIS

**Prior to Launch in Each Market:**

1. **Conduct FTO Search:**
   - Search existing patents in target country
   - Identify potential blocking patents
   - Assess infringement risk

2. **Design Around:**
   - If blocking patents found, modify implementation
   - Use alternative algorithms if needed
   - Document design-around decisions

3. **Clearance Opinion:**
   - Get patent attorney opinion on FTO
   - Document due diligence
   - Obtain insurance if high-risk market

**Estimated Cost:** $5,000-10,000 per major market

**Priority Markets for FTO:**
- Australia (before launch)
- UAE (before Middle East entry)
- US (before North American expansion)
